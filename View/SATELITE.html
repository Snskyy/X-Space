https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sputnik
SPUTNIK
Sputnik was the first artificial satellite to go into orbit, on October 4, 1957. Sputnik was launched from the Baykonur Cosmodrome, in the Kazakh SSR. The launch of the Soviet Union's Sputnik I at the height of the Cold War shocked the Western world, and caused the United States to start the Space Race.
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/EXPLORER
EXPLORER
Explorer (Alpha 1 1958) was the United States' first earth satellite, launched as part of participation in the International Year of Geophysics. This mission followed Earth's first two satellites the previous year, the Soviet Union's Sputnik 1 and 2, the start of the Cold War Space Series between the two countries.
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Palapa
PALAPA
The Palapa Satellite is an Indonesian communications satellite owned and operated by PT Indosat Tbk and launched on August 31, 2009 at 16:28 WIB at Xichang Satellite Launch Center (XSLC) using a Long March (Chang Zheng) 3B rocket. This satellite was made by Thales Alenia Space, France.
Natural Satellite
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Moon
MOON
The Moon is Earth's only natural satellite and is the fifth largest satellite in the Solar System. The Moon is also the largest natural satellite in the Solar System by the size of the planet it orbits, with a diameter of 27%, a density of 60%, and a mass of 1⁄81 (1.23%) that of Earth.
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phobos
PHOBOS
Phobos is one of the two natural satellites of the planet Mars. This satellite orbits Mars at a radius of 6,000 km. Currently, no natural satellite in the solar system orbits its parent planet this close.
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Deimos
DEIMOS
Deimos is a natural satellite smaller than Phobos, about 15 km in diameter. However, its orbit is farther from Mars (about 20,000 km) and tends to be stable, not as close to Mars as Fobos' orbit. Deimos orbits Mars with a period of 30 hours. Deimos has a flatter surface than Phobos.
